# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Engaged couples actively planning their weddings, typically on a tight budget and unable to afford a professional wedding planner. They're excited but often stressed and overwhelmed by the planning process. They come to TBWPP looking for practical guidance, reassurance, and community — someone who's been there and can walk them through it like a knowledgeable friend. The audience is diverse and inclusive: all couples, all backgrounds, all budgets.

## Product Purpose

The landing page for The Big Wedding Planning Podcast (TBWPP), a podcast with 400+ episodes, 3.5M+ downloads, running since 2016. Hosted by Michelle D. Martinez (certified wedding planner, 20+ years). The page should make visitors feel like they've found their people — then convert them to mailing list signups for updates. Secondary goals: drive podcast listens/subscribes and surface the premium subscription offering.

## Brand Personality

**Warm, real, empowering.** Heart, humor, and honesty. Michelle's voice is direct, girlfriend-like, no-BS — the friend who happens to be a professional wedding planner. The brand is anti-perfection ("perfection is a myth"), pro-community, and unapologetically inclusive. Swearing is on-brand. Aspirational but grounded — "you can do this, and we'll help."

## Anti-references

- **Pinterest-perfect wedding aesthetic** — curated, filtered, aspirational imagery that makes couples feel inadequate about their budget or choices
- **Corporate wedding industry sites** (The Knot, Zola, WeddingWire) — polished, transactional, impersonal
- **Luxury/exclusive wedding branding** — anything that signals "this isn't for you if you're on a budget"
- **Overly trendy design** — design that chases aesthetics (boho, cottagecore, etc.) and ages in 6 months
- **Generic podcast landing pages** — centered logo, Apple/Spotify buttons, nothing else

## Design Principles

1. **Real over perfect** — perfection is a myth, and the design should reflect that. Warm and human, not polished and cold. Approachable over impressive.
2. **Confidence through clarity** — couples are stressed. The page should feel like a deep breath. Clear information hierarchy, no overwhelm, easy next steps.
3. **Community, not content** — TBWPP's superpower is the community (happy hours, shared experience). The design should surface social proof and belonging, not just episodes.
4. **Inclusive by default** — the brand celebrates love in all forms. Design choices (imagery, language, color) should feel welcoming to every couple, not defaulting to any single demographic.
5. **Budget-friendly credibility** — establish authority (400+ episodes, 3.5M downloads, 20 years experience) without feeling expensive or exclusive. The expertise is accessible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Inclusive language throughout. Design for diverse representation in any imagery or illustrations. Support reduced motion preferences. Ensure strong color contrast ratios for readability.
